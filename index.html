The 6 Main Face Shapes Explained

While every face is uniquely beautiful, most fall into one of six distinct categories. Our AI technology measures the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, as well as the overall length of your face, to determine your match.

1. Oval Face Shape

The oval face is often considered the most versatile due to its balanced proportions. The face is slightly longer than it is wide, and the cheekbones are the widest part, tapering gently down to a narrow, rounded jaw. Styling Tip: You can pull off almost any hairstyle or glasses frame! Avoid heavy bangs that might unintentionally shorten your face.

2. Round Face Shape

A round face has soft, curved lines with the width and length being relatively equal. The jawline is rounded, and the cheekbones are the widest point but are not particularly prominent. Styling Tip: Opt for hairstyles that add height and volume at the top to elongate the face. Angular, rectangular glasses frames work wonderfully to add structure and contrast to the soft curves.

3. Square Face Shape

Characterized by a strong, angular jawline, a square face has roughly the same width at the forehead, cheekbones, and jaw. The overall length and width are similar. Styling Tip: Soft, face-framing layers and side-swept bangs help soften the strong jawline. Round or oval glasses frames are ideal for balancing the sharp angles of the face.

4. Heart Face Shape

Heart-shaped faces, also known as inverted triangles, feature a wider forehead and prominent cheekbones that narrow down to a distinct, pointed chin. Styling Tip: Hairstyles that add volume at the jawline, like chin-length bobs, balance the face beautifully. Aviator or bottom-heavy glasses frames help draw the eye downward, balancing the wider forehead.

5. Diamond Face Shape

The diamond shape is angular and striking, featuring high, wide cheekbones, a narrow forehead, and a narrow, pointed chin. It is the rarest face shape. Styling Tip: Hairstyles that show off the cheekbones while adding fullness at the forehead and jawline are flattering. Try rimless or oval glasses to complement your dramatic bone structure.

6. Oblong (Rectangle) Face Shape

An oblong face is noticeably longer than it is wide. The forehead, cheeks, and jawline are similar in width, and the chin may have a slight curve. Styling Tip: Bangs are incredibly flattering on an oblong face as they visually shorten the length. Waves and curls add width. Go for oversized or tall glasses frames to break up the length of the face.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is this face shape analyzer really free and accurate?

Yes, our tool is completely free to use. Our AI model has been trained on thousands of diverse faces to ensure high accuracy. It precisely identifies facial landmarks to calculate lengths and widths, offering a much more objective result than trying to measure your face yourself in a mirror.

Can my face shape change over time?

While your underlying bone structure remains the same, your perceived face shape can change slightly due to weight loss or gain, aging, or even different styling choices. However, your primary face shape classification generally remains consistent throughout your adult life.

What if I look like a mix of two shapes?

It is very common for people to have characteristics of two different face shapes (for example, a blend of Oval and Heart, or Square and Oblong). Our AI will determine your dominant shape based on the strongest mathematical ratios, but you can always borrow styling tips from your secondary shape for even more versatility.
